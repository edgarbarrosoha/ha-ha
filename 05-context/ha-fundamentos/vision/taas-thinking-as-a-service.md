# Thinking as a Service (TaaS)

**Date:** 2026-02-08
**Status:** Active — Core strategic insight
**Context:** Session 23 HA-EB, reflections between Edgar and HA-Root

---

## Core Insight

HA is not a product. HA is not an app. HA is not a consultancy.

**HA is a protocol of thinking** — a shared vocabulary and structure for humans, LLMs, and agents to navigate complexity together. Like the pentagram was for music: not the song itself, but what makes it possible for songs to exist, be shared, interpreted, taught, and evolved.

---

## Why This Matters Now

With vibecoding, building apps becomes trivial. Anyone can create an app from a prompt. The value moves from **the app** to **the thinking architecture underneath**.

HA positions at exactly that layer:
- It doesn't compete with LLMs (they are raw intelligence)
- It doesn't compete with apps (those are manifestations)
- HA is **the thinking layer** that gives structure to both

---

## Business Model: TaaS

### Layer 1: The Protocol (open/public)
- 6 dimensions, fractal structure, ha-syntax
- Usable by anyone: in Obsidian, Notion, paper, any LLM
- This is what scales virally — like markdown, like git
- **Revenue:** None directly. This is adoption fuel.

### Layer 2: Implementations (products/services)
- APIs that understand the 6 dimensions
- Templates (Obsidian, Notion, any platform)
- HA agents that "think" with the framework natively
- SIC-Q as civic implementation
- DOCET as educational implementation
- Personal vaults (ha-eb) as life implementation
- **Revenue:** SaaS, licensing, API access

### Layer 3: Consulting (high-touch)
- For those who want HA to help them think through their complexity
- Human team applies the protocol with the client
- Generates cases that validate and enrich the protocol
- **Revenue:** Project fees ($50-200K per engagement)

Money enters through layers 2 and 3. Layer 1 is what makes everything exist.

## Validation Path: Academia First

The strategy follows proven patterns:
- **Porter** (Harvard) → Five Forces → universal strategy framework
- **Design Thinking** (Stanford d.school) → universal innovation protocol
- **Agile** → manifesto → software development standard

HA follows the same playbook:

**Academia → Validation → Adoption → Standard**

### Why academic validation first:
1. **Legitimacy** — Peer-reviewed publication (Q1 Scopus) proves this isn't marketing
2. **Funding** — Opens doors to grants, research funding, institutional partnerships
3. **Adoption** — Other academics cite it, teach it, apply it. HA becomes shared vocabulary

### Why Tec de Monterrey:
- Co-author and institutional partner with weight in Latin America
- Natural channel for Edgar (professor at Escuela de Gobierno y Transformación Pública)
- Not forced — organic alignment between research and practice

### Why cases are critical:
- SIC-Q (government), DOCET (education), UPU (international) = empirical data
- Each project validates the framework in a different domain and scale
- Cases are fuel for papers, and papers are fuel for adoption

### The sequence:
**Think → Publish → Prove → Produce**
