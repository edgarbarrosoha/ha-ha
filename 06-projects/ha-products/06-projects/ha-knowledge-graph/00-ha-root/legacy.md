# Legacy: HA Knowledge Graph

Every complex system hides its structure until someone draws it. HA Knowledge Graph makes the invisible architecture of an Obsidian vault visible — every note a node, every wikilink an edge, every dimension a color.

## The Aspiration

A tool that lets anyone — Edgar, a team, a federation of HA instances — see the living topology of their knowledge. Not a static diagram. A dynamic, interactive graph that reveals:

- Which dimensions are dense, which are sparse
- Where cross-dimensional connections exist (simultaneous complexity made visible)
- How knowledge clusters form naturally
- What's isolated that shouldn't be

## The Gap

Obsidian's built-in graph view treats all notes equally. It doesn't understand HA's 6 dimensions. It can't show you that your Technology dimension has 200 notes but zero connections to Community. It can't reveal that your Learning dimension is an island. HA Knowledge Graph can.

## The Unique Angle

Edgar is both a systems thinker (HA creator) and a composer (PhD Harvard). The graph is the topology of the score — the same structure that pentagram.md describes theoretically, made computational. When you see the graph, you see the architecture of a mind organized by HA axioms.

## Connections

- **pentagram.md** — The theoretical foundation: 6 dimensions as staff lines, notes as events
- **ha-score** — Sibling project: the temporal view (time x dimension). Knowledge graph is the topological view (connections x dimension). Together they are the two lenses on the same system.
- **TaaS** — Future: knowledge graphs become API endpoints. Any HA instance exposes its topology.
- **MARA** — Future: the graph becomes the navigational interface for humans and agents.
